# The Avatar Record

The avatar record allows you to select a custom background image for your ENS name `metadata` to display in services that support it.

## What does it look like?

Here's how it looks in some popular services:
<Tabs>
<TabItem value="ENS Manager" default>

The `ENS Manager App/Site` will show your Avatar in the top-right corner, along with your wallet address.

<BrowserWindow url="app.ens.domains">
<Image img="/img/howto/v3-set-avatar-services-ens.png"/>
</BrowserWindow>

If you click it, you'll get a sub-menu where you can go to your `Profile` which shows your avatar as well as
other information set on your ENS name.

<ImageWindow title="Go to your profile">
<p align={"center"}>
<Image img="/img/howto/v3-set-avatar-services-ens-2.png"/>
</p>
</ImageWindow>

<BrowserWindow url="app.ens.domains">
<p align={"center"}>
<div style={{'background-color': "#f2f4f7", 'padding': "25px"}}>
<Image img="/img/howto/v3-set-avatar-services-ens-3.png"/>
</div>
</p>
</BrowserWindow>

</TabItem>
<TabItem value="Uniswap">

Uniswap's App will show your Avatar in the top-right corner as well!

<BrowserWindow url="app.uniswap.org">
<Image img="/img/howto/v3-set-avatar-services-uniswap.png"/>
</BrowserWindow>
</TabItem>
<TabItem value="OpenSea">

OpenSea does _not_ show your avatar the same way other sites do, because they have their own profile picture-settings,
_but_ they do show avatars set on ENS names if you go to the ENS name itself.

This is useful if you want to make your ENS name stand out on OpenSea.

<BrowserWindow url="opensea.io">
<Image img="/img/howto/v3-set-avatar-services-opensea.png"/>
</BrowserWindow>
</TabItem>
</Tabs>
